Whats wrong with trek bikes (Explained)

Nature Often
April 19, 2022
Updated on April 19, 2022

In the cycling community, you’ll sometimes hear people—particularly serious riders with high-end bikes—talk down to others about using Trek bikes.

But is there really anything wrong with Trek? Since Trek makes Bikes that span the whole gamut of bike quality and specialization, and they’re one of the world’s largest bike manufacturers, their brand gets associated in people’s minds with entry-level bikes and entry-level bikers.

In truth, Trek has nothing to be ashamed of about its products, which are well-built and retain their value for years. Some people find reasons to disagree with the values Trek associates with as a brand, like its relationship with the police or Lance Armstrong.

What’s Wrong with Trek Bikes?

Trek bikes are reliable and well-built—there are many good reasons they’re one of the world’s most popular and successful bike brands. If you’re feeling uncertain about buying a Trek bike, you shouldn’t. They’re a safe, middle-of-the-road option for whatever type of bike you’re after.

Every company has its foibles, though.

The most frequently reported mechanical problem with Treks has to do with pedal cleat systems for road bikes, which can get worn out easily. Also, the company received negative customer service reviews overall, according to one survey on Trustpilot.

Despite these foibles, Trek doesn’t deserve an across-the-board reputation for delivering a bad product or handling customer interactions poorly. Most anti-Trek sentiment has to do with the company’s status as a basic, standard, or even bland option in some bikers’ minds, or the company’s public relations stances.

Are Trek Bikes Good Quality?

Trek bikes have a deserved reputation for quality.

One reason for this is the high-quality frameset that Trek builds its bikes around.

Trek is known for making light, strong, and visually appealing frames with light metal alloys like carbon fiber or aluminum and assembling them using a special process called “invisible welding” that minimizes welding seams, often with a cool color scheme to boot.

Trek also approaches the difficult problem of frame geometry in a generalized way.

The geometry of a bike frame refers to its shape and how that shape affects your body’s positioning on the bike.

In some cases, riders must adjust the geometry of the bike themselves by selecting the correct wheel length, but Trek offers the same model of bike in different layouts.

Smaller cyclists might like a certain bike but prefer it with geometry that’s suited to them, and Trek offers that, along with frames suited to aggressive, speedy riders and relaxed, slow riders.

Common Problems with Trek Bikes

Trek is such a ubiquitous brand that asking about its common problems is like asking about the common problems with bikes in general.

Nonetheless, Trek bikes have had a major recall recently in 2021 because of widespread issues with Bontrager pedals falling off due to a technical design oversight.

This is more of a one-time problem than a deep flaw in Trek’s designs, but bikers should still hold Trek accountable for the incident, which caused some minor injuries.

Trek has a business partnership with police forces in the US, where it supplies some departments with bikes. The police came under intense scrutiny in the US in 2020, which could have brought Trek some negative publicity. Also, Trek has a PR relationship with Lance Armstrong, the world-famous cyclist who lost his Olympic and Tour de France medals in a doping case.

Do Trek Bikes Keep Their Value?

Higher-end Trek bikes are a good investment. You shouldn’t expect an entry-level Trek to be worth that much to someone else years later when they could easily afford a new one, but the higher-end Treks hold their value remarkably well. Higher-end Trek designs have stood the test of time, and some cyclists even use or refurbish Treks from decades ago.

Are Old Trek Bikes Good?

Old Trek designs have proven to be reliable and desirable, with some cyclists using frames from as far back as the 1980s. There are whole websites devoted to cataloging vintage Trek bikes since some cyclists prefer the steel frames that were popular earlier in the history of bike manufacturing.

Final Thoughts

If you hear someone at the bike shop or out on the trail disparaging Trek bikes, know that it’s probably because of some other reason than the actual quality of the bikes. People may have political disagreements or personal dislikes of the company, or they might feel like Treks are too basic of an option for them, but don’t let that give you the wrong impression of Trek.
